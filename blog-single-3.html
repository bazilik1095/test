Crafting the Future: Mastering Challenges in New Interfaces

Date: December 25
Author: Sergii Ponomarenko
Categories: Design, Branding

As technology continues its relentless evolution, the field of UI design is undergoing a transformative shift. For decades, screens held the spotlight in user interface design. However, with the rapid expansion of technology into various aspects of daily life, designers are now confronted with a diverse array of emerging interfaces.

Voice-controlled digital assistants, haptic feedback in wearables, augmented and virtual realities, gesture controls, and wearable technology are reshaping the UI design landscape. This paradigm shift demands fresh thinking and innovative approaches from designers to ensure intuitive user experiences.

Embracing the Future of UI Design

The current UI design era can be likened to the touchscreen revolution of 2007, catalyzed by the introduction of the iPhone. Yet, this new paradigm is considerably more intricate. Just as the transition from skeuomorphism to material design required time and experimentation, crafting interfaces for these emerging technologies necessitates novel blueprints and extensive trial and error. Leading design experts within the Toptal network believe that the time for designers and companies to embark on this journey is now.

Augmented and Virtual Realities: Beyond Rectangles

Immersive reality has evolved significantly since the days of Google Cardboard, which offered a rudimentary virtual reality experience. Modern immersive reality incorporates a multitude of interfaces and design possibilities, including haptic feedback, augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), advanced sound design, and voice controls, available on various commercially accessible devices.

However, merely treating immersive reality as an extension of traditional mobile app design is no longer sufficient. To harness the full potential of extended reality (XR), designers must embrace a paradigm shift. Thinking three-dimensionally and considering the interaction between the user's actual surroundings and the digital world are essential.

UI design for immersive environments requires designers to adopt an industrial design mindset, where the human body takes center stage. Questions arise: How far must an arm reach to grasp an object? What should be the average height of an object for intuitive user interaction when standing? The human body serves as the primary input mechanism for AR/VR, and hardware is continually evolving to enhance this interaction. For instance, designers are now creating medical applications that leverage AR for knee replacement surgery. Achieving submillimetric accuracy in medical procedures using AR demanded extensive experimentation and iteration. This approach led to groundbreaking applications that reduce recovery time and costs significantly.

Equipping Designers for the Future

Designers should not wait for formal guidelines to embark on projects involving emerging interfaces. It's crucial to invest time and energy in these technologies now, as the maturity of web and mobile platforms has made competition fierce and saturated.

Designers venturing into the realm of emerging interfaces can learn the principles of designing for smart environments, experiment, and collaborate with peers. Industry-standard game engines, like Unreal and Unity, are commonly used for XR experiences, with ample documentation and tools available. Designers need not be programmers, although programming knowledge can be advantageous.

Anticipating the Future of UI Design

As technology continues to advance, UI designers will face new challenges and opportunities. They must adapt to work beyond traditional screens, exploring and experimenting with emerging UIs. Organizations should ensure their teams are equipped with the latest UI tools and skills to navigate the ever-changing landscape successfully.

AR, VR, voice, gesture, and touch interfaces will open new avenues for enhancing digital and physical lives. By embracing immersive interfaces and the creative possibilities they offer, designers and companies can play a pivotal role in shaping the future of UI design. The guiding principles of interaction, product, and experiential design, coupled with a spirit of experimentation, will pave the way forward.
